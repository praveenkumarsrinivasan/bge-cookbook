# Extra Crispy Wings

## Overview

Dry-brined wings with baking powder and cornstarch, roasted hot until deeply crisp, then tossed with a simple butter and hot sauce glaze.

## Recipe Essentials

- Recipe type: Crispy roasted wings
- Cuisine: American
- Prep time: 10 minutes + 8-24 hours dry brine
- Cook time: 40-50 minutes
- Total time: 9-25 hours
- Serves: 4
- EGG setup: indirect, raised grid
- EGG target temperature: 450 F (230 C)

## Ingredients

Wings:

- 2 lb (900 g) chicken wings, split into drumettes and flats

Dry brine:

- 2 tsp (10 g) baking powder (aluminum-free)
- 2 tsp (10 g) kosher salt (use half as much by volume if table salt)
- 2 tsp (10 g) cornstarch

Sauce:

- 4 Tbsp (50 g) unsalted butter
- 2 Tbsp (30 ml) Frank's RedHot per pound of wings

To serve:

- Blue cheese dressing
- Celery sticks

## Method

1. Pat wings very dry. Toss with baking powder, salt, and cornstarch until evenly coated.
2. Arrange wings on a wire rack over a sheet pan and refrigerate uncovered 8-24 hours.
3. Set the Big Green Egg for indirect cooking with a raised grid at 450 F (230 C).
4. Roast wings until deeply browned and crisp, flipping once halfway through, about 40-50 minutes total.
5. Warm butter and hot sauce together, toss wings, and serve with blue cheese dressing and celery.

## Notes

- Use 1 tsp (5 g) cornstarch per pound of wings if scaling the recipe.
